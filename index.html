Sounds Like Me

Sharing music that I love has always felt like sharing little pieces of myself. I've chosen (with some difficulty!) just one song from each year since I got my first iPod. These are songs that can instantly transport me back to specific moments. I hope that by sharing where and when and why I first connected with them, I'll be able to provide a peek into what makes me tick.

Very often, my experience of hearing a song has a strong visual component, combining remembered POV images with colors and shapes that somehow feel like they fit the contours of the music. To that end, I've tried to present these songs in the context of equally meaningful videos, when possible.

 

2007: You Were Born For This – Tremolo

This sounds like lying awake at 2am, oscillating rapidly between excitement and terror at the prospect of starting Yale in a few weeks.

2008: Dear Confessor – Immaculate Machine

This sounds like afternoon walks up Science Hill (by way of The Cupcake Truck) before Sophomore Slump kicked in.

2009: Chicago – Sufjan Stevens

This sounds like driving back to Girl Scout camp on Sunday mornings, seeing and feeling the layers of suburbia give way to rolling hills, thick woods, and rustling fields.

2010: Adventures in Solitude – The New Pornographers*

This sounds like is one of my favorite groups of people singing my arrangement of one of my favorite songs by one of my favorite bands. It also sounds like coming home.

*As performed by Something Extra

2011: Revolting Children – The Cast of "Matilda the Musical"

When looped for several months on end, this sounds like researching and writing my MPhil thesis.

2012: Barefoot Bluejean Night – Jake Owen

This sounds like a new summer paradigm: country music, old friends, and the Baltimore Orioles.

2013: Earthquake – Labrinth ft. Tinie Tempah

This sounds like going from couch potato to ambitious boatie in a single season.
